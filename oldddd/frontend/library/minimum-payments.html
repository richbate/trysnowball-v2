Why Minimum Payments Are a Trap

When your credit card bill arrives, that “minimum payment due” can feel like a lifeline — just a small amount to keep things ticking over.

Minimum payments are designed to keep you in debt. Not to help you out of it.

💣 1. Minimum Payments Are Mostly Interest

You might pay £40, but most of that goes to interest. On a £2,000 debt at 20% APR, only £7 might go toward the actual balance.

🕳️ 2. They Create the Illusion of Progress

It feels like you’re doing the right thing, but you’re actually stuck — with interest quietly compounding every month.

🔁 3. You Stay in the Cycle Longer

Banks profit when you only pay the minimum — because it stretches your debt over decades.

🚀 So, What Should You Do Instead?

  • Always pay more than the minimum — even £20 extra helps
  • Try the snowball or avalanche method for faster progress
  • Use the What If Machine to test scenarios

Bottom line: Minimum payments keep you stuck. Break the cycle and take control of your financial future.
